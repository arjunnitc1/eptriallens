1. Introduction

You are being invited to take part in a clinical research study. Before you decide whether to participate, it is important that you understand why the research is being done and what it will involve. Please take time to read the following information carefully. You may wish to discuss it with friends, relatives, or your GP. Please ask us if there is anything that is not clear or if you would like more information.

2. Purpose of the Study

This study is investigating a new medicine called OV-201, a targeted therapy for advanced non-small cell lung cancer (NSCLC). OV-201 works by blocking specific molecular pathways (EGFR and ALK) that cancer cells use to grow and spread.

The purpose of this Phase II trial is to determine whether OV-201 is effective in shrinking tumours and slowing disease progression in patients whose cancer has continued to grow despite previous treatment with immunotherapy (such as pembrolizumab or nivolumab).

Key Fact: OV-201 has already been tested in Phase I studies involving 84 patients, where it was found to be generally well-tolerated. This Phase II trial aims to evaluate its effectiveness.

3. Why Have I Been Invited?

You have been invited because you have been diagnosed with advanced NSCLC and have previously received immunotherapy. We are looking for approximately 200 participants across 15 centres in the UK. Your oncologist has identified you as a potential candidate based on your medical history.

4. What Will Happen If I Take Part?

If you agree to participate, the following will happen:

  • Screening Visit (Week 0): We will review your medical history, perform blood tests, an ECG, and a CT scan to confirm your eligibility.
  • Treatment Phase (Weeks 1–36): You will receive OV-201 as an intravenous (IV) infusion at the clinic once every three weeks. Each infusion takes approximately 60–90 minutes.
  • Monitoring Visits: At each treatment visit, you will have blood tests and a clinical assessment. CT scans will be performed every 9 weeks.
  • Follow-Up Phase (Weeks 37–48): After completing the treatment phase, you will attend 3 follow-up visits over 3 months for ongoing monitoring.

6. Possible Side Effects

Like all medicines, OV-201 may cause side effects, although not everybody gets them.

Common (may affect up to 1 in 10 people):

  • Fatigue and tiredness
  • Mild to moderate nausea
  • Temporary changes in blood counts (detected by blood tests)
  • Mild skin rash
  • Decreased appetite

Uncommon (may affect up to 1 in 100 people):

  • Allergic reactions (including rash, itching, or swelling)
  • Liver function changes (monitored through blood tests)
  • Diarrhoea

Rare (may affect up to 1 in 1,000 people):

  • Severe allergic reaction (anaphylaxis) — emergency treatment is available at all study sites
  • Interstitial lung disease

7. Possible Benefits

We cannot promise that the study will help you directly, but the information we get from this study may help improve the treatment of people with NSCLC in the future. Some participants in Phase I studies experienced tumour shrinkage with OV-201.

8. Your Rights as a Participant

Voluntary participation: Taking part in this study is entirely voluntary. You are free to withdraw at any time, without giving a reason. This will not affect the standard of care you receive.

Confidentiality: All information collected about you during the course of the research will be kept strictly confidential. Your data will be anonymised and stored securely in accordance with UK GDPR and the Data Protection Act 2018.

Withdrawal: If you withdraw from the study, the data collected up to that point may still be used in the analysis (unless you specifically request otherwise). You will continue to receive standard medical care.

9. Expenses and Payments

The study treatment, related tests, and clinic visits are provided at no cost to you. Reasonable travel expenses will be reimbursed upon submission of receipts (up to £50 per visit). No other payments are made for participation.

10. What If Something Goes Wrong?

If you are harmed as a result of taking part in this study, there are no special compensation arrangements. If you are harmed due to someone's negligence, you may have grounds for legal action. Regardless of this, if you wish to complain about any aspect of the study, you can contact the Patient Advisory Liaison Service (PALS) at your hospital.
