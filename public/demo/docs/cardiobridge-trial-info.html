1. Introduction

You are being invited to take part in a research study. Before you decide, it is important for you to understand why the research is being done and what it would involve. Please take your time to read the following information carefully and discuss it with others if you wish. Ask us if there is anything that is not clear or if you would like more information.

2. Purpose of the Study

This study is evaluating the CardioBridge System, a new minimally invasive cardiac support device designed for patients living with chronic heart failure (CHF). The device is intended to help the heart pump blood more efficiently without requiring open-heart surgery.

This Phase III trial aims to determine whether the CardioBridge System is safe and effective compared to optimal medical therapy alone. The study will assess improvements in heart function, exercise capacity, quality of life, and hospitalisation rates over a 12-month period.

Key Fact: The CardioBridge System has been tested in Phase I and Phase II studies involving 156 patients. Earlier studies showed promising improvements in cardiac output and exercise tolerance, with an acceptable safety profile. This Phase III trial is the final step before seeking regulatory approval.

3. Why Have I Been Invited?

You have been invited because you have been diagnosed with chronic heart failure (NYHA Class III or IV) and your medical team has determined that you are not a suitable candidate for conventional cardiac surgery. We are recruiting approximately 350 participants across 22 centres in the UK and Europe.

4. What Will Happen If I Take Part?

If you agree to participate, the following will happen:

  • Screening Visit: A comprehensive cardiac assessment including an echocardiogram, blood tests, a 6-minute walk test, and quality-of-life questionnaires.
  • Randomisation: You will be randomly assigned to one of two groups:
    • Group A: CardioBridge device placement + optimal medical therapy
    • Group B: Optimal medical therapy alone (control group)
  • Device Placement (Group A only): The CardioBridge device is placed using a minimally invasive catheter procedure. This is typically performed under local anaesthesia with sedation and takes approximately 1–2 hours.
  • Follow-Up Visits: All participants attend follow-up visits at 1 week, 1 month, 3 months, 6 months, and 12 months after enrolment (or procedure date for Group A).

6. Possible Risks and Complications

Risks related to the CardioBridge device placement (Group A):

  • Bleeding or bruising at the catheter insertion site
  • Infection at the insertion site (treated with antibiotics)
  • Blood vessel damage during the catheter procedure
  • Cardiac arrhythmias (irregular heartbeats) during or after placement
  • Device malfunction or displacement (rare — occurred in less than 2% of previous study participants)

Risks related to sedation/anaesthesia:

  • Allergic reactions to sedation medications
  • Temporary drowsiness and nausea

Risks for all participants:

  • Discomfort from blood tests and assessments
  • There is a possibility that the treatment may not provide a benefit

7. Possible Benefits

If you are assigned to Group A and the device works as hoped, you may experience improvements in your heart function, exercise ability, and overall quality of life. Even if you do not benefit directly, the information gained from this study will help improve the treatment of heart failure in the future.

If you are assigned to Group B, you will receive comprehensive monitoring of your heart failure as part of the study, which may exceed the frequency of your routine NHS follow-up.

8. Your Rights as a Participant

Voluntary participation: Taking part in this study is entirely voluntary. If you decide not to take part, or if you decide to withdraw at any time, this will not affect the quality of the medical care you receive.

Confidentiality: All data collected about you will be anonymised and stored securely in compliance with UK GDPR. Your GP will be informed of your participation (with your permission) so they can take this into account in your care.

Crossover option: If you are assigned to Group B, and the study results are positive, you may be offered the CardioBridge device after the study ends, subject to regulatory approval.

9. Expenses and Payments

All study procedures, including the device placement, are provided free of charge. You will be reimbursed for reasonable travel expenses to attend study visits (up to £75 per visit). No other financial compensation is provided for participation.

10. Insurance and Indemnity

CardioBridge Medical Research has arranged insurance to cover any harm that may arise as a direct result of participating in this study. If you believe you have been harmed by taking part, please contact the research team immediately.
